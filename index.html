----The Land of Kohinoor

Telangana, formed on June 2, 2014, is India's 29th state with Hyderabad as its capital. It is a land rich in history, home to iconic landmarks like Charminar, Golconda Fort, and Warangal Fort. The state is known for its vibrant culture, festivals like Bathukamma and Bonalu, and the world-famous Hyderabadi Biryani. With a glorious past rooted in the Kakatiya dynasty and the Nizam era, Telangana proudly blends tradition with modernity. Telangana sits in the heart of South India, bordered by five states, and is blessed with fertile lands, rivers like Krishna and Godavari, and rich natural resources. The state is also a major IT hub, with Hyderabad being home to global tech giants like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon. Telangana's folk art, classical music, and traditional Pochampally silk weaves reflect the deep cultural roots of its people. The famous Kohinoor Diamond, once the most prized gem in the world, traces its origins to the Golconda mines right here in Telangana Telangana is one of India's fastest-growing economies, driven by a booming pharmaceutical industry that earns it the nickname "Bulk Drug Capital of the World" the city of Hyderabad alone produces nearly 40% of India's pharmaceutical exports. The state is home to some of India's most impressive engineering marvels, including the Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project, one of the largest multi-stage lift irrigation projects in the world, designed to bring water to drought-prone regions. Telangana's cuisine goes far beyond biryani — dishes like Sarva Pindi, Gongura Mutton, and Jonna Rotte reflect the rustic, bold flavors of its agrarian heartland. The state has a strong sporting culture, producing champions in kabaddi, cricket, and boxing. The Pro Kabaddi League team Telugu Titans proudly represents the region on the national stage. Telangana is also an emerging destination for eco-tourism, with reserves like Nagarjunasagar-Srisailam Tiger Reserve — one of the largest tiger reserves in India — drawing wildlife enthusiasts from across the country. The people of Telangana speak Telugu, one of the oldest classical languages in the world, with a literary tradition stretching back over a thousand years.


----HISTORY OF TELENGANA

Telangana's history dates back thousands of years, with its earliest roots traced to the Satavahana dynasty around 230 BCE, who ruled the Deccan region and made it a thriving center of trade and Buddhism. Later, the powerful Kakatiya dynasty, with their capital at Warangal, ruled from the 12th to 14th century and left behind magnificent architectural marvels like the Warangal Fort and the famous Thousand Pillar Temple. After the fall of the Kakatiyas, the region came under the Bahmani Sultanate and later the Qutb Shahi dynasty, who founded Hyderabad in 1591 and built the iconic Charminar. The Golconda Fort became the center of the world's most famous diamond trade, producing legendary gems like the Kohinoor and the Hope Diamond. In the 18th century, the Asaf Jahi dynasty, popularly known as the Nizams, took control and ruled Hyderabad as one of the wealthiest princely states in British India, leaving behind a rich legacy of grand architecture, culture, and arts.

----Cultural Highlights

Telangana’s culture is a rich blend of traditions and modern influences, making it one of the most vibrant states in India. The state is famous for Bathukamma and Bonalu festivals, which showcase its community spirit and devotion to local deities. Its heritage includes the Kakatiya temples, the mighty Golconda Fort, and the iconic Charminar in Hyderabad, all of which stand as symbols of its glorious past. Telangana is also known for Pochampally Ikat sarees and Cheriyal scroll paintings, reflecting its artistic excellence and traditional craftsmanship. The world-renowned Hyderabadi biryani and the fusion of Urdu-Telugu literary traditions add to its unique cultural identity. The state’s folk arts are equally captivating, with Perini Shivatandavam, a warrior dance from the Kakatiya era, and Oggu Katha, a storytelling tradition accompanied by music, keeping alive the oral traditions of Telangana. The rhythm of folk songs in Telugu, blended with Urdu poetry and Sufi music, creates a cultural harmony that is unique to this land. Telangana is also celebrated for its handicrafts such as Bidriware metal craft, Nirmal paintings, and Dokra figurines, which showcase the creativity of local artisans and preserve centuries of cultural heritage. Food is another cornerstone of Telangana’s identity, with rustic delights like Sarva Pindi (spiced rice flour pancake), Jonna Rotte (sorghum bread), Kodi Kura (chicken curry), and sweets like Qubani ka Meetha and Double Ka Meetha reflecting the influence of Persian and Mughal cuisines.

----POPULAR TURIST SPOTS

Telangana offers stunning natural attractions and vibrant modern destinations. The spectacular Kuntala Waterfalls, the highest in the state, is surrounded by lush forests and is a favorite picnic spot. The scenic Pochera Waterfalls near Adilabad cascade beautifully into rocky terrain. The tranquil Pakhal Lake, built during the Kakatiya era, is a serene getaway with forests and wildlife around it. The vast Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is one of the largest masonry dams in the world, offering boat rides to the island museum of Nagarjunakonda. The peaceful Pocharam Wildlife Sanctuary is home to deer, wild boars, and migratory birds. The Kawal Tiger Reserve is a major eco-tourism spot, protecting tigers, leopards, and rich biodiversity. The Hussain Sagar Lake in Hyderabad is famous for its giant Buddha statue and boating activities. The lively Ramoji Film City, the world’s largest film studio complex, is a fun-filled destination for families and cinema lovers. The Birla Mandir, built with white marble, offers panoramic views of Hyderabad and Hussain Sagar. The Chowmahalla Palace dazzles visitors with its regal halls and chandeliers, showcasing the grandeur of the Nizams. The Salar Jung Museum houses one of the world’s largest collections of art, artifacts, and antiques. The Pochampally village, known as the “Silk City of India,” is famous for its handwoven Ikat sarees. The Basar Saraswati Temple is a unique pilgrimage site where children are initiated into learning. The Alampur Jogulamba Temple, one of the 18 Shakti Peethas, is a revered spiritual destination.

----POPULAR CUISINE

Telangana food is bold, spicy, and deeply rooted in its culture. Staples like rice, jowar, and bajra form the base of everyday meals, while the generous use of red chilies, tamarind, and sesame seeds gives dishes their fiery and tangy character. The crown jewel of Telangana cuisine is the Hyderabadi Biryani, slow-cooked with fragrant spices and tender meat, a dish that has earned global fame. Another local favorite is Sarva Pindi, a savory rice flour pancake that is crispy, wholesome, and uniquely Telangana. The famous Gongura Mutton, made with sorrel leaves, delivers a sour punch that perfectly balances the richness of meat. Rustic staples like Jonna Rotte, a sorghum flatbread, highlight the state’s agrarian roots, while Mirchi ka Salan, a nutty and tangy gravy made with green chilies, is often served alongside biryani. Telangana cuisine also features Kodi Kura (spicy chicken curry), Pachi Pulusu (raw tamarind soup), and Bagara Baingan (stuffed brinjal curry), each dish showcasing bold flavors and traditional cooking methods. Sweet delicacies such as Qubani ka Meetha, made from dried apricots, and Double Ka Meetha, a bread pudding with rich Mughal influence, add a touch of indulgence to festive occasions. The cuisine reflects the fusion of local traditions with Persian and Mughal culinary styles, making Telangana’s food not just a meal but a cultural experience that celebrates its heritage, diversity, and love for spice.

Political Landscape

Telangana politics has shifted from BRS dominance since 2014 to Congress leadership today, making it one of India's most dynamic political battlegrounds. Telangana is the youngest state in India, carved out of the erstwhile Andhra Pradesh on June 2, 2014, fulfilling a decades-long demand by the people of the region. Hyderabad serves as its capital and is one of India's most important technology and business hubs, home to major global companies like Microsoft, Google, Amazon and Apple. The state has 33 districts and a population of around 3.5 crore people.
