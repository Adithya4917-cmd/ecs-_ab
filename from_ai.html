Overview

Heart of the Deccan

Telangana — formed June 2, 2014

Telangana, formed on June 2, 2014, is India's 29th state with Hyderabad as its capital. It is a land rich in history, home to iconic landmarks like Charminar, Golconda Fort, and Warangal Fort. The state is known for its vibrant culture, festivals like Bathukamma and Bonalu, and the world-famous Hyderabadi Biryani.

"The famous Kohinoor Diamond traces its origins to the Golconda mines right here in Telangana."

Telangana is also a major IT hub, with Hyderabad being home to global tech giants like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon. It earns the nickname "Bulk Drug Capital of the World" — Hyderabad alone produces nearly 40% of India's pharmaceutical exports. The Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project, one of the largest multi-stage lift irrigation projects in the world, stands as a modern engineering marvel.

Heritage

A Thousand Years of History

From ancient Mahajanapadas to a modern state, Telangana's story spans over two millennia of dynasties, culture, and resilience.

700 BCE

Aśmaka Mahajanapada

One of the sixteen great kingdoms of ancient India, flourishing along the Godavari River.

230 BCE

Satavahana Dynasty

Ruled the Deccan for nearly four centuries, promoting Buddhism, trade, and irrigation.

12th–14th C

Kakatiya Dynasty — Golden Age

Capital at Warangal. Built the Warangal Fort, Thousand Pillar Temple, and promoted Perini Sivatandavam warrior dance.

1591

Qutb Shahi Dynasty

Founded Hyderabad and built the iconic Charminar. Golconda Fort became the world's diamond trade center — birthplace of the Kohinoor and Hope Diamond.

18th C

Nizam Era

Asaf Jahi dynasty ruled as one of the wealthiest princely states in British India. Legacy: Chowmahalla Palace, Falaknuma Palace, Osmania University.

2014

India's 29th State

After decades of struggle, Telangana was officially born on June 2, 2014, with Hyderabad as its capital.

Tourism

Popular Tourist Spots

Telangana offers stunning natural attractions and vibrant modern destinations. The spectacular Kuntala Waterfalls, the highest in the state, is surrounded by lush forests. The vast Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is one of the largest masonry dams in the world.

  • Hussain Sagar Lake — Giant Buddha statue & boating
  • Ramoji Film City — World's largest film studio complex
  • Birla Mandir — White marble, panoramic Hyderabad views
  • Chowmahalla Palace — Regal Nizam grandeur
  • Salar Jung Museum — World's largest single-person collection
  • Pochampally Village — Silk City of India
